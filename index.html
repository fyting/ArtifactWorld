Abstract

3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) delivers high-fidelity real-time rendering but suffers from geometric and photometric degradations under sparse-view constraints. Current generative restoration approaches are often limited by insufficient temporal coherence, a lack of explicit spatial constraints, and a lack of large-scale training data, resulting in multi-view inconsistencies, erroneous geometric hallucinations, and limited generalization to diverse real-world artifact distributions.

In this paper, we present ArtifactWorld, a framework that resolves 3DGS artifact repair through systematic data expansion and a homogeneous dual-model paradigm. We establish a fine-grained phenomenological taxonomy of 3DGS artifacts and construct a comprehensive training set of 107.5K diverse paired video clips. Architecturally, we unify the restoration process within a video diffusion backbone, utilizing an isomorphic predictor to localize structural defects via an artifact heatmap, which guides restoration through an Artifact-Aware Triplet Fusion mechanism. Extensive experiments demonstrate state-of-the-art performance in sparse novel view synthesis and robust 3D reconstruction. Code and dataset will be made public.

Method

ArtifactWorld uses a video diffusion backbone with a defect-localization branch (artifact heatmap) and Artifact-Aware Triplet Fusion so spatio-temporal repair stays aligned with corrupted regions while preserving multi-view coherence—conceptually similar in spirit to “wide video + clear sectioning” on project pages like ArtiFixer, but for 3DGS artifact restoration rather than NVS extrapolation.
